1. Overview About Screenshots on Mac#

Taking screenshots on your Mac while scrolling is an incredibly useful way to capture long web pages and other content. This quick guide will help you understand how to use the built-in Mac screenshot tool to create scrolling screenshots.

To begin, open the screenshot tool by pressing the shift-command-5 keys and select “Options”. Under the options tab, you can choose to capture the entire window or a portion of the screen. To capture a scrolling screenshot, make sure to select “Capture Entire Scrolling Window” and then press “Capture”.

Your Mac will now capture the entire scrolling window as one image. The image will be stored in your “Screenshots” folder in the Finder and will be labeled with a date-time stamp. If you would like to edit the image before saving, you can use the built-in markup tool to crop and annotate the image.
