2. Modifying Battery Settings#

  1. Adjust your auto-lock settings. This setting determines how long your phone will wait before it automatically locks your display. By reducing the amount of time your phone is idle, you can reduce the amount of juice being used by the display. To adjust your auto-lock, go to Settings > Display & Brightness > Auto-lock.
  2. Dim your display. Your phone’s display eats up a ton of battery juice, so dimming it can help save some battery. You can adjust the brightness in the same place as auto-lock. Try to keep it at the lowest level that’s comfortable for you.
  3. Change your background. Dark backgrounds can help your iPhone’s battery last longer. Try switching out your bright wallpaper for a darker one to save battery.
  4. Modify your battery settings. When your battery is low, the Low Power Mode feature automatically kicks in. While this does save battery, it limits a lot of your phone’s performance. To optimize your battery settings, go to Settings > Battery > Battery Health. Here you can turn on Optimized Battery Charging which will help you get the most out of your battery.
