1. Turn off the device#

Turning off your device can help to clear and reset the system caches. To turn off your Samsung Galaxy S7 device, press and hold the power button for a few seconds. You will see the shutdown dialog asking you to confirm your choice. Select “Power Off” and wait for the device to turn off. After it is completely shut off, press and hold the power button for a few seconds to turn it back on. This will help to clear the system caches stored in your device.

Next, open the Settings menu. You will find it in the Apps drawer. Scroll down to the bottom and select “Storage & USB”. Once you are in the Storage & USB menu, select “Cached data” and tap on the option that appears. Select “Ok” in the pop-up to clear all the cached data from your device.

Third, you can clear the app caches from the Apps menu. Find the Apps drawer and open it. Then select the application you want to clear the cache for. Once you are in the Application info screen, select “Storage”. You will see the “Clear Cache” option. Tap on it to delete the cached data from the app.

Fourth, you can clear the browser caches. On your Samsung Galaxy S7 device, open the browser and go to the Settings menu. Here, select the option for clearing caches. This will clear out all of the saved data from your browser.

Finally, restart your device. This will help to ensure that all of the cleared caches and data are no longer stored on your device. Restarting the device will also help to improve the speed and performance of the device.
