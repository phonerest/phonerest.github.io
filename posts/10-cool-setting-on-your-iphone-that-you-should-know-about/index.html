Q1: What are the 10 cool settings on iPhones that I should know about? A1: The 10 cool settings on iPhones that you should know about include Automatic Updates, Background App Refresh, Wi-Fi Assist, Password Autofill, iPhone Battery Management, App Permissions, Airplane Mode, App Limits, Sleep Schedules, and AirDrop.

Q2: How do I enable Automatic Updates on my iPhone? A2: To enable Automatic Updates on your iPhone, go to Settings > General > Software Update > Automatic Updates. Turn the toggle switch to the on position to enable Automatic updates.

Q3: What is Background App Refresh and how does it work? A3: Background App Refresh is a feature on iPhones that allows apps to refresh their content in the background when connected to Wi-Fi or data. This allows apps to update their content even when you’re not using them, so you have the most up-to-date content when you open them.

Q4: How does Wi-Fi Assist work on my iPhone? A4: Wi-Fi Assist is a feature on iPhones that allows your device to switch to cellular data from Wi-Fi when Wi-Fi connectivity is weak. This helps reduce draining your battery by staying connected to a weak Wi-Fi signal. To enable Wi-Fi Assist, go to Settings > Cellular > Wi-Fi Assist.

Q5: What is the difference between Airplane Mode and Sleep Schedules? A5: Airplane Mode turns off all connections to Wi-Fi, cellular data, and Bluetooth to help conserve your battery and avoid disruptions. Sleep Schedules set a schedule for when your iPhone will go into Do Not Disturb mode to reduce notifications, phone calls, and messages.
