ii. Setting Up Screen Time on an Android Phone#

Screen time monitoring is an important part of managing our phone usage. Android phones come with built-in features that help parents monitor their children’s screen time. With Android, you can easily set limits, lock down app usage, and set alarms to help manage their screen time. You can even monitor the apps they are using and the amount of time they’re spending on each app. Here’s how to check your screen time on an Android phone. First, open the Settings app on your phone and click on Digital Wellbeing & Parental Controls. You can also adjust the amount of time allowed for certain apps and set limits on when they can and can’t use them. Second, you can also set time limits for certain apps by tapping the “Set time limit” button. This will allow you to specify how long the app can be used in a day, and also set a daily limit for total screen time. Third, you can also use alarms to help manage screen time. This will help remind your child when it’s time to take a break from their phone. To set an alarm, open the Settings app and click on Alarm & Sounds. Here, you can set an alarm for a certain time of the day or for when a certain amount of time has passed. Fourth, you can also block certain apps from being used if you want to limit their usage. To do this, open the Settings app and click on Apps & Notifications. From here, you can select the app you want to block and toggle the “block app” option. This will prevent the app from being used until you unblock it.

In conclusion, Android phones provide several features to help you monitor your child’s screen time. You can check their app usage, set time limits, set alarms, and even block apps if necessary. This way, you can ensure that your child is using their phone responsibly and not spending too much time on it.
