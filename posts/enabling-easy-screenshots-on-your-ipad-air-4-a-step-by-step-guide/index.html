A1: To enable easy screenshots on your iPad Air 4, press and hold the top button (aka the Power button) and the Home/Touch ID button at the same time. When the screen flashes, you should know that your screenshot has been taken.

Q2: How do I access my screenshots after I take them?

A2: After taking a screenshot, you can access it in your Photos app. Open the Photos app and select the Albums tab at the bottom. Here, you should see a folder called Screenshots, which houses all of your screenshots.

Q3: What if I accidentally delete a screenshot?

A3: If you accidentally delete a screenshot, you can access it in the Recently Deleted folder in the Albums section of the Photos app. Select the Recently Deleted folder and open it. Here, you will be able to recover any screenshots you have deleted.

Q4: Can I take a screenshot of a game or other app?

A4: Yes, you can take a screenshot of a game or other app. Simply press and hold the top button (aka the Power button) and the Home/Touch ID button at the same time and the screenshot will be taken.

Q5: Are screenshots stored on my iPad Air 4 or iCloud?

A5: The screenshots you take using your iPad Air 4 are stored locally on the device. However, you can also store the screenshots in iCloud by enabling iCloud Photos in the Settings app.
