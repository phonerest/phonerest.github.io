1. Definition & Uses of QR Code#

QR Codes, short for Quick Response Codes, are two-dimensional barcodes used to store data. It was first developed in Japan for the automotive industry. QR Codes are now used in many industries, from retail to marketing, from healthcare to transportation. With the help of a smartphone camera, you can scan a QR Code to get information about the item or service associated with it. In this tutorial, you’ll learn how to scan QR Codes on your Android device.

There are a few ways you can scan QR codes on Android depending on what device you have. The most popular way is to use a third-party QR Code scanning app. These apps are readily available on Google Play Store, and free to download. Once downloaded, the app will open up and you’ll be able to scan the QR Code by pointing your device’s camera at it.

Many Android devices also come with a built-in QR code scanner. You can open up the camera on your device, select the Settings button, and enable the QR Code scanning feature. Once enabled, you can point the device’s camera at the QR Code and it will automatically scan the code.

Finally, some Android phones have a dedicated QR Code scanning button. This button is usually located on the side of the device. You can press the button and your device will open up the QR Code scanner. Point the camera at the code and it will scan it quickly and accurately.

QR Codes are a convenient and quick way to share and store data. With the help of a QR Code scanner, you can easily scan and decode these codes on your Android device. So go ahead and explore the world of QR Codes today!
