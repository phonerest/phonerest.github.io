1. How to Monitor Your Android Device’s Total Screen Time#

With the rise in technology, screen time has become an important factor in our lives. And managing that screen time requires an efficient system that reduces distractions and helps us track our device usage. Android provides an efficient way to monitor and limit your device’s total screen time with its built-in Digital Wellbeing feature. Here’s how to make use of it:

Firstly, open your device’s main Settings menu and select Digital Wellbeing and parental controls. Then, select “Dashboard” from the menu at the top. Here you’ll be able to see the amount of time you’ve spent on your device, including the number of times you’ve unlocked your phone. Additionally, you can set a daily limit for yourself.

If you want to further limit your device’s screen time, you can also turn on “Wind Down”, which will turn on Night Light and Do Not Disturb when you reach your daily limit.

Finally, you can also set up “Focus Mode”, which will turn off your apps when you reach your daily limit. This will help you focus on the task at hand and keep distractions away.

Android’s Digital Wellbeing feature is an efficient way to monitor and limit your device’s total screen time. You can easily set a daily limit, turn on “Wind Down” and “Focus Mode”, and get timely updates on your device usage.
