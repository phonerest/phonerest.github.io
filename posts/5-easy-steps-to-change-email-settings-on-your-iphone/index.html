2. Configure the Mail App#

If you want to change email settings on your iPhone, it can be done easily in just five steps. First, open the Mail app and select the Settings gear icon at the bottom of the screen. Once in settings, select the account you would like to change settings for. After selecting the account, you can change server information, default alert sound, email signature, and more.

You can also configure default Mail app behavior, like how often the app checks for new emails and how many emails are shown in each message list. To do this, select the ‘Fetch New Data’ option under the ‘Mail’ heading. From here, you may choose to disable Push, enable Fetch, or set a custom schedule for the mail app.

In addition to configuring the Mail app, you may also change the alerts for each account. To do this, select the ‘Notifications’ option for each account under ‘Accounts’. Once in Notifications, you can adjust the alert style, sound, and whether or not notifications show a preview.

You can also choose to receive Time-Sensitive Alerts. This is useful for emails that have an important due date or are from a trusted sender. To enable this feature, select the ‘Show in Notification Center’ option under the ‘Alerts’ heading.

Finally, you may manage all of the email accounts you have connected to your iPhone in the ‘Accounts’ screen. Here you may add, delete, or change the settings of your email accounts. With these steps, you’ll be able to easily change email settings on your iPhone.
